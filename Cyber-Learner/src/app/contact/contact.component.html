Try to Report a Cyber Attack or Scam

Dealing with a cyber attack or scam can be overwhelming, but you are not alone. We have compiled a list of trustworthy resources in Brisbane, which can aid you in reporting cyber attacks and recovering losses. If you are a child, you should notify your parents or guardian and have them help if you have fallen victim to a cyber attack.

Scamwatch - Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC):

Website: Scamwatch
URL: https://www.scamwatch.gov.au/report-a-scam
Description: Scamwatch is a national resource that provides information on various types of scams, how to report them, and tips to avoid falling victim to scams. The link above is for reporting a scam.

Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC):

Website: ACSC
URL: https://www.cyber.gov.au/
Description: ACSC offers advice and support to people in Australia to enhance their cybersecurity awareness and respond to incidents related to cyber crime.

eSafety Commissioner:

Website: eSafety Commissioner
URL: https://www.esafety.gov.au/
Description: The eSafety Commissioner focuses on online safety, including dealing with online abuse, cyberbullying, and other online safety concerns.

Community-Based Cybersecurity Support Groups:

You may also have local cybersecurity groups, where you and your family can go to receive help. A quick online search may help you find them.
