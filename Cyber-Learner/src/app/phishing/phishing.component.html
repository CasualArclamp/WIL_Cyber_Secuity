What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which one or more targets are contacted by email, phone, or text message by someone pretending to be a legitimate authority to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, bank and credit card details, and passwords.

Common features of Phishing Emails

  • 1. Deceptive Sender Address: Phishing emails may display a sender address that appears legitimate at first glance, but upon closer inspection, it may contain slight misspellings, unusual domains, or random characters.
  • 2. Generic Greetings or No Personalization: Phishing emails often use generic greetings like "Dear Customer" or do not address the recipient by name. Legitimate organizations typically use personalized greetings.
  • 3. Urgent or Threatening Language: Phishing emails may create a sense of urgency or use threatening language to pressure recipients into taking immediate action, such as claiming an account will be suspended.
  • 4. Spelling and Grammatical Errors: Phishing emails may contain noticeable spelling and grammatical mistakes, which are less likely to be present in communications from reputable organizations.
  • 5. Mismatched URLs: Hovering over hyperlinks in a phishing email may reveal that the displayed link does not match the actual destination. Attackers often use disguised URLs to redirect victims to malicious websites.
  • 6. Request for Personal Information: Phishing emails commonly request sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, Social Security numbers, or account credentials, which legitimate organizations typically do not request via email.
  • 7. Attachments or Suspicious Links: Phishing emails may contain attachments or links that, if opened or clicked, could download malware or lead to a fake website designed to steal information.
  • 8. Unusual Sender Behavior: The sender's behavior may be suspicious, such as unexpected requests for sensitive information, unusual language, or communication outside of normal channels.
  • 9. Unsolicited Attachments: Phishing emails may include unsolicited attachments, especially those with file types commonly associated with malware (e.g., .exe, .zip).
  • 10. Misspelled Brand Names: Cybercriminals often impersonate well-known brands or organizations but may misspell the name slightly, which can be a telltale sign of a phishing attempt

    How to prevent Phishing attacks?

    Verify Sender Information:

    Always double-check the sender's email address, especially if the message contains suspicious content or requests.

    Do Not Click on Suspicious Links:

    Hover over links to view the actual URL before clicking. Be cautious of shortened URLs or URLs that seem unrelated to the content of the message.

    Be Skeptical of Urgency:

    Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency. If you receive a message pressuring you to act quickly, take extra precautions to verify its legitimacy.

    Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    Enable MFA whenever possible for your accounts. This provides an additional layer of security even if your password is compromised.

    Keep Software Updated:

    Regularly update your operating system, applications, and security software to ensure they are protected against known vulnerabilities.

    Install and Use Security Software:

    Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software to help detect and prevent phishing attacks.

    Beware of Pop-Up Windows:

    Avoid clicking on pop-up windows or dialog boxes in emails, especially if they ask for personal information.

    Guard Personal Information:

    Never provide sensitive information like passwords, social security numbers, or financial details in response to unsolicited messages.

    Verify Requests for Payment or Sensitive Data:

    If you receive a request for payment or sensitive information, verify it through a separate communication channel (e.g., a phone call) before taking any action.

    Enable Spam Filters:

    Use email services with strong spam filters to help identify and quarantine phishing emails.

    Report Suspected Phishing Attacks:

    If you receive a suspected phishing email, report it to your email provider and to the Anti-Phishing Working Group (reportphishing@apwg.org).

    Monitor Accounts Regularly:

    Keep a close eye on your financial accounts, social media, and email accounts for any unusual activity.
